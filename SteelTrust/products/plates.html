Plates

Mild Steel or M.S. (ASTM A-36, SS400)

  • ASTM (American Society for Testing and Materials) A36 is the most commonly used mild and hot-rolled steel. It has excellent welding properties and is suitable for grinding, punching, tapping, drilling and other machining processes. SS400 material is under the Japanese Industrial Standard (JIS). Quality and components are similar and almost the same as A36, application and usage are also interchangeable in most cases.
  • Mild steel plates usually come in 1219mm, 1524mm, 1829mm, and 2438mm widths and thickness ranges from 3mm to 50mm. Other size thicknesses and customized dimensions are also available with corresponding lead times to produce or import.

ABS (Grade A)

  • ABS (American Bureau of Shipbuilding) steel plates are almost exclusively utilized in the shipbuilding industry- for the construction of structural parts of ships, barges and marine equipment.
  • ABS plates usually come in 1829mm width and thickness ranges from 4mm to 50mm. Other size thicknesses and customized dimensions are also available with corresponding lead times to produce or import.

Checkered (ASTM A-36)

  • Checkered plates are characterized by a distinct waffle-like or raised diamond pattern surface. They are usually used for walkways for platforms.
  • Checkered plates usually come in 1219mm width and thickness ranges from 1.8mm to 6mm. Other thicknesses are also available with corresponding lead times to produce or import.

Flat Bar (ASTM A-36)

  • Flat bars are a flat, rectangular section with square edges, usually of mild steel material, varying in sizes. This can be used for a wide variety of applications and its customized sizes make them suitable and cost-effective.
  • Flat bars usually come in 20ft or 6m in length. Thicknesses usually start from 2mm and widths are most of the time custom-cut according to requirements.

Base Plate (ASTM A-36)

  • This is usually a cut-to-size mild steel material used as a supporting plate or frame at the base of a column to provide increased stability. Dimensions and thickness will be dependent on requirements.

Boiler (ASTM Grade 516 or Grade 70)

  • A carbon steel grade with specific chemical requirements to be used in pressure or boiler vessels.
  • Boiler plates usually come in 1829mm width and thickness start from 6mm and up. Other thicknesses are also available with corresponding lead times to produce or import.

Abrasive Resistant (AR SS-55)

  • AR plates have been developed specifically for long service life in harsh conditions. AR products are suitable for a variety of applications in areas such as mining/quarrying, conveyors, material handling and construction, and earthmoving.
  • AR plates usually come in 1829mm width and thickness start from 6mm and up. Other thicknesses are also available with corresponding lead times to produce or import.

Carbon Steel (ASTM A-283 Grade C)

  • Carbon steel plates are usually of use in riveted, bolted, or welded construction of bridges and buildings, and for general structural purposes. Carbon steel and mild steel’s usage can often interchange but note that the latter has less carbon content.
  • Carbon steel plates usually come in 1829mm width and thickness start from 6mm and up. Other thicknesses are also available with corresponding lead times to produce or import.
